Title, Summary and Team Members

  • Playing God: Procedural Solar System Generation
  • We will procedurally generate a solar system complete with a sun, planets (rocky ones, oceanic Earth-like ones and gaseous giants) and moons, some of them with their own atmospheres. We plan to do this in Unity (3D).

Problem Description

  • Here you should provide the context for your idea. Describe the problem that you are trying to solve, why it is important, where it is challenging. Give us a general idea on how you are going to solve it.
  • In our project, we hope to create a randomly generated solar system complete with the physics of each planet in respect to a star. Although Sebastian Lague’s videos teach us how to generate rocky planets, we would also like to generate gaseous planets.
  • The problems that we are trying to solve are how to make a realistic-looking gaseous planet model, how to generate semi-realistic planets through random generation, and creating an interactive model of the final solar system. Solving these problems is important to creating models of planets and solar systems that we cannot otherwise see with our eyes in space.
  • One of the challenges that may arise in this project is simulating what atmospheres look like around planets without obscuring the planet’s structure. We hope to research what atmospheres and gases would look like surrounding a planet before we move forward with its implementation.
  • Another challenge we may have is making our simulation interactable. Although Unity will most likely have helpful elements that will make this possible, we will still need to work with the cameras to make the simulation accessible to anyone who uses it.  

Goals and Deliverables

  1. We want to
  1. Procedurally generate a solar system like the one shown above
  1. With customizable parameters (e.g. number and types of planets, stars, etc)
  1. Be able to move around and explore the system
  2. Each planet itself will have randomly generated terrain (including oceans, mountains and biomes)
  3. Some of them will be gas giants
  4. Some will have atmosphere (like Earth and Venus), which will change how the terrain underneath looks
  1. We believe we can accomplish this because we have a guide for planet generation in the YouTube series listed in the resources section. The challenges will be adding atmosphere and different textures for gas giants and stars, hooking up the physical simulation, and making a camera and world navigation system that allows you to explore the solar system. But leveraging the Unity engine, we suspect that these deliverables are within reach.
  2. Our baseline plan is to have atmospheric (1) and gaseous (2) planets with terrain (3) orbiting around a star (4). If we have time, we can make the solar system more gamelike by adding a navigation mechanism (5) allowing you to traverse the system and zoom in/out and rotate around the planet surfaces.
